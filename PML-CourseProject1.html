Predicting how barbell weight lifting exercise is performed.

Synopsis

Barbell exercises provide an easy and effective way of targeting every major muscle groups in your body. there are more then 100 ways to perform Barbell weight lifting. Each method is concentrated on different muscle.

Human Activity Recognition (HAR) The goal of HAR is to analyze the data available and recognize what activity the person under test is performing. Human activity recognition becoming an important area of computer vision research. Its applications include surveillance systems, patient monitoring systems, and a variety of systems that involve interactions between persons and electronic devices.

In recent years Internet of Things is gaining critical mass and many Wearable devices with sensors are no more confined to science fictions they are reality and soon become integral part of human lives in develop countries.

One great example for wearable devices with fitted sensors is Fitness tracking devices. There are more then 25 Fitness Tracking devices commercial devices available for public today ( 20th June 2014). These Fitness Tracking wristband can track various human activities and body records such as heart rate etc.

One thing that people regularly do is quantify how much of a particular activity they do, but they rarely quantify how well they do it. Main objective of this analysis is to analyse data generated from accelerometers on the belt, forearm, arm, and dumbell of 6 participants while they perform barbell lifts in 5 different ways and predict which method of barbell they are performing. 4. Data splitting

Cleaned training data is further split in to train and validate data sets. My intention is to use train dataset to train my prediction model and validate it using validation dataset. modelValidate = cleanedTrainingData[-trainingIndex,]
